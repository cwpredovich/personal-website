Bio

Growing up in California, technology was ever-present and constantly evolving all around me. I discovered that my passion for helping people and working with technology could grow and allow me to grow into new, exciting and more challenging roles. Writing scripts, small programs, and building websites had been a hobby of mine for many years, but when a close friend told me that I could make web (app) development a career, things started to change. I was doubtful at first, but we continued to talk about it.

He told me about his journey: attending a notable university and studying Computer Science for a few semesters, realizing that he wanted to find a faster way to get into a career, and then discovering a coding bootcamp nearby. He worked hard in the bootcamp, went on to work for the school as a tutor for several months, and then eventually landing his first full time programming job.

This had all happened only one year before our conversation. I had heard about coding bootcamps, but I was so skeptical. How was it possible that someone could learn enough to get a job in only a few months? I had to know more. I asked him to teach me what he knew. He taught me the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. He even let me tour his workplace where he wrote backend code in NodeJS.

I was hooked. I knew that I wanted to learn more. I started taking online courses (including Codecademy) and learning as much as I could. I was working full time, so I had to make time to learn. I would practice coding during slow periods at work, I would study on my lunch breaks, and I would study after work. I was determined to learn as much as I could. I was learning so much about data types, conditionals, and loops, and I was having so much fun. I knew that doing this long-term would make a fulfilling career.

Meanwhile, I researched coding bootcamps and found one that I liked. I applied, got accepted, and started the program in the summer of 2018. Many hours, many late nights, many programming-bugs-that-almost-made-me-pull-my-hair-out later, I proudly graduated from the program in the fall of 2018. I had learned so much in such a short amount of time. I had built several projects, I had learned how to work with a team, and I had learned how to learn.

Since then, I have worked on several projects, including a front-end project aimed at improving workplace efficiency for Verizon by unifying multiple internal applications into one place. I have also worked on a full-stack project for CoFi that speeds up the payroll process which construction contractors and sub-contractors have to go through, called the draw process. Most recently, I've been working on a full-stack app for a startup called Skillco where we're empowering college students and alumni to find meaninful careers.

I'm still learning and building, and now I'm looking for a new opportunity. I am excited to see where this journey takes me, and I am excited to see how my passion for technology will expand and flourish in the future.
